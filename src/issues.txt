Note: When we press CTRL+\ while running our minishell under valgrind, the signal path gets
complicated. Normally, SIGQUIT goes directly to our shell, but with make v, valgrind becomes
the parent process that receives the signal first, then forwards it to our minishell. Our shell
correctly ignores it (we set signal(SIGQUIT, SIG_IGN) for interactive/heredoc modes), but 
valgrind's process wrapper doesn't fully isolate the signal, so the make command also sees
it and prints its own "Quit (core dumped)" message. This causes double messages and terminal
corruption.
